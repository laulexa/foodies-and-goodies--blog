Asadero Prime

When I visited Asadero Prime for the first time, a friend of mine suggested it to me and kept talking about how delicious it was. I'm originally from Colombia, and I've never been to a Mexican steakhouse. The enormous statue of a Catrina, a representation of the Day of the Dead, caught my attention as soon as I walked into the restaurant. As soon as you step inside the restaurant, this lovely sculpture can communicate Mexican culture and create a welcoming atmosphere.

I was unsure of what to order despite the menu's abundance of mouthwatering items, from Mexican nachos to top-notch steak cuts. My attention was drawn to the "Zabuton Wagyu," a meat cut served on a sizzling hot stone with chimichurri tortillas and a delicious spicy sauce. I kept ordering the same dish every time I went to Asadero because the Wagyu Beef was so flavorful and tender.

The La Carne Asada, on the other hand, was what my husband tried; this meat was served with tortillas, chorizo, nopal (a cactus paddle), and sauces. It was also a fantastic dish. I really enjoy this restaurant because they offer a wide selection of sauces after you place your order. When compared to other steakhouses, this one had great prices, great service, and a ton of value for the time spent there.

I highly suggest that you go here if you're looking for a great steakhouse, love sauces, and are open to trying something new. It will undoubtedly make you fall in love!
